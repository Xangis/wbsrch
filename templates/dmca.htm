DMCA and Copyright Policy

Per our Inclusion Policy, we have no interest in indexing sites whose sole purpose is to facilitate copyright infringement. We care about intellectual property, and even though copyright law in the United States is incredibly broken, we believe content creators should be able to exercise control over and profit from the works that they have created. To that end, we will comply with DMCA takedown requests in most cases.

Since our crawler is automated, sites are added to our index without being reviewed by a Human, and content that we do not approve of can and does make it into the index. We accept no responsibility for content on other websites that you may find or access when using our products or services.

It is our policy to publish a copy of every legal notice that we receive. We may also send a copy to the Chilling Effects Project (http://www.chillingeffects.org) or The Electronic Frontier Foundation (http://eff.org) for publication, if applicable. We may also send a copy of the notice to the supposed infringer or, if we question the validity of your complaint, to the rightsholder.

Notice for Claims of Intellectual Property Violations and Agent for Notice

This is for copyright and intellectual property matters only. Correspondence about other matters will be discarded.

If you believe that your work has been copied in a way that constitutes copyright infringement, or that your intellectual property rights have been otherwise violated, please provide the Agent for Notice with the following information in English in your notice:

  • A description of the copyrighted work or intellectual property that you claim has been infringed
  • A description of where the material that you claim is infringing is located on our site, with enough detail that we may find it on the website (give us a URL).
  • An electronic or physical signature of the person authorized to act on behalf of the owner of the copyright or other intellectual property interest.
  • A statement by you that you have a good faith belief that the disputed use is not authorized by the copyright or intellectual property owner or its agent.
  • A statement by you, made under penalty of perjury, that the above information in your Notice is accurate and that you are the copyright or intellectual property owner or authorized to act on the owner's behalf.
  • Your mailing address, email address, and telephone number.
